Taste & Treasures

Authentic flavors and unique keepsakes found only in the Kingdom.

Jordanian Cuisine

Mansaf (The National Dish)

A Bedouin feast of lamb cooked in a sauce of fermented dried yogurt (Jameed) and served with rice. You eat it with your right hand!

Zarb (Bedouin BBQ)

Meat and vegetables buried in an underground oven with hot coals for hours. You will likely eat this for dinner in Wadi Rum.

Knafeh

A rich dessert made with thin noodle-like pastry soaked in sweet sugar-based syrup and layered with cheese. Best eaten warm in downtown Amman.

Limonana

The ultimate refresher: Frozen lemon juice blended with obscene amounts of fresh mint.

What to Bring Home

Dead Sea Products

The mineral-rich black mud and bath salts are world-famous for skin health. Much cheaper to buy here than imported abroad.

Mosaic Art

Madaba is the city of mosaics. You can buy incredible hand-made mosaic plates or frames directly from the workshops there.

Shemagh (Red Scarf)

The traditional red-and-white checkered scarf. Practical for the desert sun and a symbol of Jordanian heritage.

Za'atar & Sumac

Visit a spice market (Souq) for high-quality Za'atar (thyme blend) or Sumac (a citrusy red spice used in salads).

April Packing List

  • Layers:

    Amman/Petra can be cool (15°C) in evenings, while Aqaba/Dead Sea will be hot (28°C). Bring a light fleece and t-shirts.

  • Footwear:

    Sturdy hiking boots/sneakers for Petra. Water shoes (essential!) for Wadi Mujib and Dead Sea (rocky salt crystals).

  • Modesty:

    Loose trousers/skirts and covered shoulders are respectful in cities. Swimwear is fine at resorts and Wadi Mujib.
